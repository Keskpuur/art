“Roosad koogid turu”

Priisle Market, which closed in 2014 symbolizes the bittersweet nature of change and the feeling of not recognizing the places where you once grew up. The pink-glazed cakes, with their vibrant, sugary tops, evoke a sense of a bygone era, a time that is now faded but still carries a sense of longing.

Plans to rebuild the Central Market are set to begin in 2025. Now is your chance to savor a slice of nostalgia. Later, it just will not feel the same.

Curator: Kaur Järve

supported by the Cultural Endowment of Estonia.
